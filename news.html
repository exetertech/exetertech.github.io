Why You Should Consider Not Using Antivirus Programs

Antivirus programmes have been around for a while now and are supposed to protect your computer from threats. In theory this is a great idea and it can flag up viruses and trojans but at what cost?

At ETS we have seen antivirus programmes often causing more issues than they solve. Common side affects of an antivirus programme can include; intermittent/complete loss of internet connectivity, other 3rd party programmes not working as they should and a general sluggish speed of doing anything, as antivirus programmes can often run in the background and consume a surprising amount of processing power.

Technology news outlet Ars Technica did a brilliant piece on this exact issue a whilst back. At ETS we feel it continues to be a relevant issue and one that will continue for the foreseeable future, for as long as computer manufacturers strike deals with antivirus companies and electronics retailers.

This isn’t just obscure companies that are the culprit here, these are major antivirus manufacturers. Even the eponymous McAfee antivirus software often causes Wi-Fi connectivity issues and is often difficult to uninstall. For help uninstalling McAfee we have supplied some links to the McAfee uninstaller here.

The first thing you should do after setting up your computer is check for any preinstalled antivirus programmes and remove them. 

The solution? If your using a Windows PC, it’s Windows Defender. It’s not perfect (nothing is) and of course is no substitute for vigilance and a healthy dose of scepticism but it’s one of the best and by far the most convenient options available.

a) It’s made by Microsoft for Windows- thus less conflicts with the system.

b) Totally free. Yep thats right, no subscription fees or purchase fees.

c) It’s already there. All you need to do is uninstall any antivirus thats already there.

Beware the preinstalled antivirus

Even if an antivirus is not active, just being on the system can be problematic. The first thing you should do after setting up a new computer is removing any 3rd party antivirus. This reduces the chance of any issues and allows windows defender to be activated.

Nothings perfect

Nothing is going to be an absolute solution and it’s good to take precautions to ensure you don’t get viruses.

If your going to run antivirus, use one, only one!

Using more than one antivirus can cause a myriad of issues. If you have to use one, make sure that you don’t have any others that could conflict. Windows Defender will usually automatically deactivate if another antivirus is installed.

An exception is Malwarebytes & Windows Defender. Malwarebytes is aimed more towards stopping malware than viruses. The way Malwarebytes is coded allows it to run alongside Windows Defender without an issue in most cases. 

Many popular antivirus programmes have security flaws

Antivirus programmes often have flaws of their own, if these are exploited it can be a larger surface area to attack for hackers and a potential way in.

Mac users

Apple products have a built in firewall but no built in antivirus. Overall Mac’s are not targeted as much as Window’s computers, but if you decide to get an antivirus/anti malware programme, Sophos and Malwarebytes tend to work well on most Apple computers as a precautionary measure.
